Currently all furgason.com email is hosted by a company called IONOS (formerly 1and1). This has been a reliable and inexpensive service for more than 10 years. Unfortunately, the service limits the number of email addresses and the size of the email storage quota. To upgrade it to support more than 5 accounts and more storage would cost more than $350/yr. which isn't worth it to me. I would like to offer anyone that wants a furgason.com email address the ability to have one as well as expand the email quota limit.

Unfortunately moving the mail server requires that all email accounts are moved at one time

No, you will not lose any email in the transfer. I have tested the process multiple times and verified it works well. In addition, your email will safely remain in the current email servers through the migration and for an extended period of time after the migration.

While I have tested this process many times and I am very confident of it's success, I am not guaranting the process is flawless. Like anything computer related that is critical, you should have a backup.

The process takes place in 5 steps.

  1. New accounts will be created on the new email server. It is recommended that you utilize the same password for your new account as your old one (at least during the conversion) as it will make the process simplier. Once the transfer is complete you may use the webmail interface to change your password to whatever you would like.
  2. For each account, all email will be synced between the old and new servers. This initial syncing process will take from a few minutes to a couple of hours the first time it runs, depending upon the number of messages and size of the mailbox.
  3. The (DNS) servers on the internet that indicate how to locate the furgason.com mail server will be updated to point the mail server address to the new mail host. This step will instruct email servers to deliver mail to your account to the new email servers. The old email accounts will still remain on the old server but they will no longer receive any new email.
  4. The new email servers will being receiving new email. The old and new email servers will be synced one additional time to ensure that any email copied from the original sync is updated on the new server. You will receive an email when this step has been completed. I would recommend that you perform a quick validation that everything looks to be in working order. If everything appears to be in working order, I'd recommend changing your email account password. This step it not required but it's good practice (and I really don't want to know your email account password).
  5. Assuming there are no issues with the migration, I will delete the old email accounts on the old hosting provider about 30 days after the migration is completed. Once that has been completed there will not be any way to recover any old email from your account so it's important to verify your new account is working as soon as possible after the conversion.

In your email client configuration, you'll need to check to see which address you have configured for your Incoming IMAP server and Sending SMTP server. The names your email client calls these servers may be slightly different but you should have something close to this in your configuration.

If your setting point to imap.1and1.com, smtp.1and1.com, imap.ionos.com or smtp.ionos.com you will need to change the address of your mail servers. The new address for both the IMAP and SMTP servers should be mail.furgason.com. The port for IMAP is 993 and the port for SMTP is 587. These should not need to be changed as they are the same ports that are used with the current mail server.

If any of these settings are incorrect, I would recommend that you change them now. I have configured mail.furgason.com to point to the old email server and I will change it to point to the new one of the day of the migration.
